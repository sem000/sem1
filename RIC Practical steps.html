Practical 7 Sampling is the process of selecting a subset of individuals from a larger population to estimate certain characteristics of that population. Random sampling is done by generating a random sample of individuals from the population, usually by using random number generators or a table of random numbers. This method is relatively simple and inexpensive, and it is widely used in social and natural sciences research.One of the main advantages of random sampling is that it reduces the chances of bias, as every individual in the population has an equal chance of being selected. Stratified sampling is a statistical method of sampling in which the population is divided into smaller groups, called strata, based on certain characteristics. The goal of stratified sampling is to ensure that the sample represents different subgroups of the population in proportion to their representation in the population. This can be beneficial when different subgroups within the population have different characteristics or when the population has a non-random distribution of certain characteristics.
